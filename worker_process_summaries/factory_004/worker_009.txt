Possible Process Optimizations

Workstation Layout and Material Presentation
The worker currently pulls loose shirts from an unorganized pile on the folding table and occasionally retrieves shirts from piles on the floor near sewing machines. Presenting incoming garments in raised, organized carts or gravity-fed bins at waist height would eliminate bending, reduce material handling time, and standardize the staging process. 

Material Handling and Transport 
During the sorting process, the worker transfers stacks of packaged garments between cardboard boxes resting directly on the floor. This requires repetitive deep reaching and bending. Elevating the boxes on pallets, lift tables, or waist-high sorting racks would significantly reduce ergonomic strain. 

Process Sequencing and Double Handling (Inference)
It is inferred that transferring packaged garments from one box to another represents a redundant sorting or consolidation step. If the packaging line could sort and pack garments directly into their final shipping boxes based on color or order destination, this secondary box-to-box transfer process could be eliminated entirely. 

Process Consolidation (Inference)
The worker currently performs quality inspection/thread trimming at one table and ironing/folding at another. It is inferred that these loose garments must be moved between these stations. Integrating the thread snipping and lint removal into the start of the ironing/folding cycle—or placing the inspection station directly adjacent to the folding station via a slide or conveyor—could reduce the transport and staging of loose inventory between the two tasks.
