*   **Elimination of Intermediate Staging (Inference):** The worker frequently moves small stacks of folded or packaged items from their primary workstation to secondary staging tables, and later moves them again into the wire-mesh cart. Placing the wire-mesh cart directly adjacent to the active folding/packing station would eliminate redundant material handling and reduce travel time.
*   **Ergonomic Final Boxing Station:** The final boxing process requires the worker to assemble boxes on the floor and repeatedly bend down to transfer items from the cage cart into the boxes. Providing an elevated packing table or a box-holding stand at waist height would eliminate repetitive bending, improving the ergonomic flow and speed of the packing process.
*   **Workstation Information Access (Inference):** The worker frequently interrupts the folding process to walk to an office desk and use a phone receiver/scanner. If this is a required system interaction (such as logging batches or checking work orders), providing a mobile scanner or placing a terminal directly at the folding workstation would significantly reduce unnecessary travel time.

Establishing a dedicated material handler (water spider) or using gravity-fed flow racks to deliver raw materials and remove finished carts could keep the worker focused on the primary value-add tasks of folding and packing.
*   **Tape Dispenser Management:** During final boxing, the worker repeatedly picks up and sets down a handheld tape dispenser. Using an automated case sealer or a mounted, heavy-duty tabletop tape dispenser could streamline the box-sealing step.
